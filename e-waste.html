E-waste

Computers and laptops

Old computers, laptops, and computer accessories should never be placed in household recycling bins. These electronic items contain valuable materials such as metals and plastics that can be safely recovered through specialised e-waste recycling programs. Proper recycling also helps prevent harmful substances from entering the environment while supporting responsible disposal practices.

Mobile phones and tablets

Mobile phones, tablets, chargers, and electronic accessories can often be recycled through local e-waste collection services and electronic recycling programs. Many devices contain reusable materials including lithium, copper, and aluminium that can be processed and reused in new products. Recycling electronic devices also helps reduce landfill waste and protects natural resources.

Televisions and monitors

Televisions, computer monitors, and display screens require specialised recycling due to the materials and components used in their construction. These items may contain glass, metals, and electronic circuits that should be handled safely by approved recycling facilities. Proper disposal helps reduce environmental harm while supporting sustainable electronic waste management.

Small household electronics

Small electronic items such as keyboards, printers, gaming consoles, headphones, and cables can also be recycled through e-waste programs. Even small devices contain recyclable materials that can be recovered and reused instead of being sent to landfill. Separating electronic waste from general household waste supports more effective recycling systems and improves resource recovery.

Community E-Waste Collection Program Expands Local Recycling Access

Our community e-waste collection initiative provides households and businesses with safer and more convenient ways to dispose of old electronic devices. The program focuses on reducing electronic waste sent to landfill while improving public awareness about responsible recycling practices. Community members can access educational resources and information about approved recycling locations for computers, televisions, mobile devices, and other electronic items.

Schools Introduce E-Waste Awareness and Recycling Activities

Schools are increasingly incorporating e-waste education into environmental learning programs to help students understand the importance of responsible electronic disposal. These activities include classroom discussions, recycling projects, and awareness campaigns focused on reducing electronic waste and protecting the environment. By teaching students about e-waste recycling, schools encourage sustainable technology habits and greater environmental responsibility.

Businesses Improve Sustainability Through Electronic Waste Recycling

Businesses are adopting more sustainable e-waste management practices by recycling outdated office equipment and reducing unnecessary electronic waste. Workplace recycling initiatives help organisations safely dispose of computers, printers, and electronic accessories while supporting environmentally responsible operations. Through improved recycling systems and staff awareness programs, businesses can reduce landfill contributions and support long-term sustainability goals.
